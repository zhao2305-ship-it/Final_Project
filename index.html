Final Project – Water Pinball

A real-time 3D water simulation combined with a small pinball-style game.

What this project is about

This project started as a water surface simulation that focuses on waves, lighting, and how objects under the water look distorted.

During development, I decided to add interaction to make the demo more engaging. In the final version, a ball is launched into the scene and continuously bounces off the walls and a center cube.

Every collision creates strong ripples on the water surface. The goal is to guide the ball into the exit area within 15 seconds.

How to interact

  • Mouse left drag: rotate the camera
  • Mouse wheel: zoom in and out
  • Left / Right arrow keys: adjust launch angle
  • Enter: launch the ball or restart
  • R: reset the game
  • 1 / 2 / 3: change wave strength
  • ESC: quit

Connection to the class

The project uses time-based animation, GLSL shaders, and basic lighting models. The underwater image is sampled as a texture and distorted based on water motion, creating a refraction-like effect.

Files and build

Required files:

  • simple_water.cpp
  • Hachimi.bmp (must be in the same folder)

Build and run (Windows, MSYS2 UCRT64):

cd /d/Final_project
g++ simple_water.cpp -o water.exe ^
  -lmingw32 -lSDL2main -lSDL2 -lglew32 -lopengl32
./water.exe
